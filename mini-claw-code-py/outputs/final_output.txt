At the Edge of Quiet

At the edge of quiet a small light turns,
Like a careful promise folded into dawn.
It moves among the cups and scattered learns
To lift the weight of night and carry on.

A paper plane remembers how to fly,
Tracing patient arcs across the morning air.
We watch the slow, polite untying of the sky,
And find the ordinary tender there.

Keep this small hour like a single coin,
Softly spent, returning something bright.
In pockets warmed by hands that never join,
The day begins again from little light.
