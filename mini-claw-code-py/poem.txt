Evening's Quiet

The last light folds into a careful hush,
A ribbon of gold slipping from the day.
Windows keep their small bright vows of warmth,
While distant trains forget the route of time.

A single lamp spills secrets onto the floor—
A map of shadows, soft as folded hands.
Memory walks in measured, patient steps,
Collecting the small brave things we've left behind.

Outside, the city exhales, then settles down;
The stars, indifferent, begin their slow applause.
We listen for the language of return,
And find, between the heartbeats, a gentle home.
