New Light

A thin ribbon of light across the sill,
A quiet rumor that the dark will go.
Paper cups hold yesterday's last warm spill,
While curtains count the slow return of glow.

Footsteps stitch the floor with small intents,
A kettle sings the morning into speech.
Outside the map of sky rearranges cents,
The day unbuttons pockets within reach.

We gather up the ordinary threads—
Loose coins, a note, the print of someone’s hand.
In simple rooms where ordinary beds
Keep gentle watch, the world becomes a land.
